What is Phishing?

A phishing attack is a type of cyber attack where an attacker tries to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card details, by pretending to be a trusted entity, usually through deceptive emails, messages, or websites.

Learn how to protect yourself from phishing attacks by recognizing suspicious emails, websites, and messages.

Phishing Email Examples

Example 1: Suspicious Email

Dear User,
Your account has been compromised. Please click here to reset your password.

Hint: Check the email address and URL before clicking any link!

Example 2: Fake Bank Email

Dear Customer,
We have detected unusual activity in your bank account. Please click here to secure your account.

Hint: Banks never ask for sensitive information via email!

Phishing Quiz

Question 1: You receive an email from an unknown sender asking you to reset your password. What should you do?

Question 2: An email claims you’ve won a prize and asks for your bank details. What should you do?

Question 3: You receive a message from a trusted contact asking for your personal information but with a suspicious tone. How should you respond?

Question 4: A website you visit asks for your login credentials, but the URL looks strange. What should you do?

Question 5: You receive an email from your company’s IT department asking you to verify your account details. What should you do?

Question 6: An email from a friend asks you to check out a cool link, but it looks suspicious. What should you do?

Question 7: You receive an SMS from your bank asking for your password to confirm a transaction. What should you do?

Question 8: A pop-up on a website claims your computer is infected with malware. What should you do?

Question 9: A job offer email asks you to send personal information such as your social security number. What should you do?

Question 10: You get a friend request on social media from someone you don't know. What should you do?
